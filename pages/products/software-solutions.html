Design Principles

Know Your Audience

Different users need different information - executives need high-level summaries, managers need departmental metrics, operators need real-time data, and analysts need detailed access.

Show What Matters

Focus on metrics that drive decisions, indicators that require action, trends over raw numbers, and context for understanding data. Avoid information overload.

Make It Visual

Well-designed visualizations communicate faster than tables, highlight important information, show relationships between metrics, and make trends immediately apparent.

Keep It Real-Time

Stale data leads to poor decisions. Design systems that update automatically, show data freshness, handle delays gracefully, and provide historical context.

Enable Interaction

Allow users to filter and drill down, adjust time ranges, compare periods, and export data for further analysis. Static reports have limited value.

Optimize Performance

Efficient data queries, strategic caching, responsive design for different screen sizes, and print-friendly layouts when needed ensure reports are usable and accessible.
